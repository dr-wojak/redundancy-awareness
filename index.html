— README · Redundancy Awareness

These files are essential for any legal review or Public Accounts Committee audit. They provide the timestamped proof required to shift the burden of proof to the State regarding fiduciary neglect. The 2025 Summary of Enquiry is the most significant legally: it transforms a failure to act into a documented refusal to govern.

Four key components of the record
I · Finality of Notice
12 months. No decision pathway.
Documentation confirming that the Office of the Tánaiste remained in possession of high-level strategic warnings for over twelve months without initiating any decision pathway. Notice provided; receipt confirmed; action: nil.
II · Designed Forgetting
Systematic loss. Re-submission required.
A chronological mapping showing how evidence submitted in 2024 was systematically lost in the administrative architecture, requiring the 2025 re-submission archived here. Evidence absorbed; retrieval refused; the archive grows; nothing converts.
III · Jurisdictional Dead-Ends
Séachanachas — governance by avoidance.
Records of the circular referral process, where the enquiry was bounced between departments to avoid substantive engagement — providing a live case study of Séachanachas: the institutional art of avoidance elevated to administrative policy.
IV · Trigger for Statutory Filing
The "Policy Disagreement" defence is void.
This file serves as the immediate precursor to the Ethics in Public Office Act filings. It establishes that the State refused to acknowledge the scientific basis of the proposal at all — let alone disagree with it. The defence of substantive disagreement is therefore unavailable.

Legal Footnotes & Statutory Authorities
Four pillars · GDPR-compliant redactions
Article 15.10 · Bunreacht na hÉireann
Constitutional petitions. The right to petition the State under Art. 15.10 grounds the formal submissions to the Houses of the Oireachtas archived in this repository.
Article 28 · Bunreacht na hÉireann
Collective responsibility. The requirement for the Government to act on strategic risks known to its members. Once any Cabinet member was formally notified, the duty attached to the collective executive.
Ministers and Secretaries Acts
Defines the legal obligation of Ministers to manage the business of their departments with necessary foresight. The Acts provide the mandamus ground for compelling performance of statutory duty.
GDPR · Redacted Personal Dispatches
Documents within this repository have been redacted to remove sensitive personal identifiers of non-public figures while maintaining evidential integrity. Redactions comply with GDPR and privacy obligations while preserving the structural value of the public record regarding ministerial conduct.
